Tonight's plan

Run of Show 📋

Here's how the evening flows. Promise it won't be too long — the goal is to celebrate, play, and leave everyone buzzing.

🎉

Welcome & Kick Off

~5 mins
Uncle B

Welcome everyone — parents, kids, the whole family. Remind everyone what Code Quest was, what the kids set out to do two weeks ago, and how far they've come. Set the mood: this is a celebration, not a test.

🔵

Tech Bros — Demo

~10 mins
Theodore · Ezekiel · Arthur

The boys show their quiz. One of them hosts, parents and family join as players using game code BROS. Everyone plays a round together. After the demo, each boy gets 30 seconds to say one thing they learned or built.

💖

Girl Power — Demo

~10 mins
Elsie · Hannah · Sophia

The girls show their quiz. One of them hosts, everyone else joins using game code GIRL. Another full round together. Each girl gets 30 seconds to share what they're most proud of.

🏅

Badge Ceremony

~10 mins
All kids

Uncle B reveals each kid's personal badge — one by one. Each badge has a name and a reason. This is the moment to make them feel truly seen for what they specifically brought to the bootcamp. Emma gets hers too! 🌟

🗣️

Kids Speak — What Did You Learn?

~8 mins
All kids

Each kid answers one simple question: "What's one thing you know now that you didn't know two weeks ago?" Could be technical, could be about teamwork, could be anything. No wrong answers — the point is to hear it in their own words.

🔮

What's Next — Code Quest v2

~5 mins
Uncle B

Plant the seed for what could come next. Bigger projects, real products, more skills. Let the kids dream out loud about what they'd build if they could build anything. Keep it light — the goal is to leave them excited, not overwhelmed.

👑

Parents Judge — Bragging Rights Decided

~5 mins
Parents

The parents deliberate and announce the winning team. Judging criteria: design, questions, gameplay, features, and how well they presented. Winner gets the most important prize in the Olatoye family — bragging rights. 😂

🥂

Celebrate!

For the judges

What to Look For 👀

Parents — here's what you're judging on. But remember: both teams built something real. Whatever you decide, everyone wins tonight.

🎨

Design

Does it look amazing? Is it fun, colourful and easy to use? Does the design feel like it belongs to the team who made it?

🤔

Questions

Are the quiz questions good? Are they interesting, original, and the right difficulty? Did the team put thought into what they asked?

🎮

Gameplay

Does it work properly? Is it fun to play? Does it feel like a real game — not just a list of questions on a screen?

⚡

Features

Did they add anything extra and impressive? A leaderboard? A timer? Animations? Famous quotes? Show-off features count!

🗣️

Presentation

Can they explain what they built and how? Do they show pride in their work? Can they answer questions about it?
