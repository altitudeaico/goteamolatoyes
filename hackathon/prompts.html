Your words are your superpower

Claude is incredibly powerful — but it can only build what you describe. The clearer and more detailed your words are, the more amazing the result will be.

Think of it like ordering food at a restaurant. If you say "food please" you might get anything. If you say "a large pepperoni pizza with extra cheese, crispy base" — you get exactly what you want! 🍕

🔑 The one big rule

"The more specific you are, the better Claude's answer will be."

Let's look at some examples. Same request — but one prompt is vague and one is detailed. See the difference:

"Make me a webpage"
"Make me a webpage about me — my name is Elsie, I love horses and drawing, my favourite colour is purple, and I want it to have shooting stars in the background and my name in big glittery letters at the top"
What Claude will do: Make a basic white page with "Hello World" or a generic layout — it doesn't know anything about you!
"Make a quiz"
"Make a 10-question quiz about Premier League football for kids aged 8-14. Multiple choice with 4 options each. Show a scoreboard at the end. Make it exciting with football emojis and green and white colours."
What Claude will do: Make a random quiz about something — you have no idea what topic, how many questions, or what it'll look like.

✏️ The Prompting Formula

Every great prompt has three ingredients. Mix them together and Claude can build almost anything:

🧪 The Recipe for a Great Prompt

🎯
1. What you want Be clear about what you're asking for. "Make a webpage" vs "Make a personal webpage about me"
📋
2. The details Tell Claude everything it needs to know — your name, your topic, your colours, your ideas
🎨
3. How you want it to look or feel Colourful? Dark? Fun? Professional? With animations? With emojis? Tell Claude the vibe!

📜 4 Golden Rules

🔍
Be specific Don't say "make it nice" — say "make it purple with gold stars and big bold text"
🧩
Give context Tell Claude who it's for — "for an 8-year-old", "for my whole family to play", "for the hackathon"
📐
Say the format Do you want a list? A webpage? A story? Code? A quiz? Always say what type of thing you want
🔄
Ask for changes If you don't like something, just say so! "Make the font bigger", "change the colour to blue", "add more questions"

💪 Weak vs Strong — More Examples

😬 Weak
"Write me a story"
Claude has to guess everything — the character, the setting, the plot, the length...
🔥 Strong
"Write me a 3-paragraph adventure story about a 10-year-old girl who finds a magical horse in her school playground. Make it exciting with a cliffhanger ending."
Claude knows exactly what to write — you'll love the result! ✅
😬 Weak
"Make my page better"
Better how? Claude doesn't know what "better" means to you...
🔥 Strong
"Add an animated rainbow background to my page, make the heading font bigger, and add a section at the bottom with my top 3 favourite songs with music note emojis 🎵"
Crystal clear — Claude can do exactly this! ✅
😬 Weak
"Explain coding"
Too broad — Claude might write an essay that's too long or too complicated...
🔥 Strong
"Explain what HTML is in 3 simple sentences that an 8-year-old would understand. Use a fun analogy."
Short, simple, with a fun comparison — perfect! ✅

🚀 Try these prompts right now!

Copy these into Claude and see what happens. These are all examples of strong prompts — notice how detailed they are!

🎨 Design something:

Design a logo for my team called [YOUR TEAM NAME]. Use [YOUR FAVOURITE COLOUR] as the main colour. Make it look powerful and fun. Describe what it looks like and show me the design as an SVG image.

📖 Get a story:

Write a short funny story (about 5 sentences) where [YOUR NAME] and their best friend discover a secret door in their school that leads to a world made entirely of [YOUR FAVOURITE FOOD]. Include one joke and end with a surprise twist.

🧠 Learn something:

Explain how the internet works in exactly 4 bullet points. Each point should be one sentence. Use simple words that a 10-year-old would understand. Start each bullet with a fun emoji.

🎮 Build something:

Make me a simple clicker game in HTML. Every time I click a big button, I earn a point. Show my score at the top. After every 10 clicks, show a funny celebration message with confetti. Make it colourful and mobile-friendly.
🎯 Challenge: After you try one of these, go back to Claude and ask it to change something. Use the 4 golden rules — be specific about exactly what you want different!

📌 Quick Cheat Sheet — Save This!

1
Start with what you want "Make me a...", "Write me a...", "Build me a...", "Explain..."
2
Add your details Name, topic, colours, style, who it's for, how long/short
3
Say the vibe "Fun and colourful", "dark and mysterious", "simple and clean", "exciting with animations"
4
If you don't like it — say so! "Make the text bigger", "change the colour", "add more detail", "make it shorter"
5
Magic words that always help "Keep it simple", "make it fun", "suitable for all ages", "show me the full code", "give me 3 options"
