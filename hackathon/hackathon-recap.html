In their own words

What They Learned 🧠

On the night, each kid answered one question: "What's one thing you know now that you didn't know two weeks ago?" Here's what they said.

💬

How to prompt AI clearly

The most important skill of the whole bootcamp. Not code — words. Clear, specific, detailed prompts get results. Vague prompts get vague results. Every kid learned this the hard way at least once.

🗄️

What a database actually is

Two weeks ago Hannah asked "what is a database?" Tonight she has one running behind a live multiplayer quiz. That's the journey in one sentence.

🌐

How to put something on the internet

GitHub Pages, repositories, pushing code, live URLs. They understand the difference between something that lives on your screen and something that lives on the internet. That's a real skill.

👑

Confidence and clear presentation

Elsie said it best — it's not enough to build something good. You have to be able to show it and talk about it with confidence. That lesson will go way beyond coding.
