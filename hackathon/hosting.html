What does "putting it online" actually mean?

When Claude makes your webpage, it only exists on your screen — like drawing a picture in your notebook. Nobody else can see it.

Putting it online means giving your page its own real website link so Mum, Dad, cousins, everyone can visit it from anywhere in the world!

And it's completely free 🥳

⭐ The Easy Way — Ask Uncle B!

✅ Start here — this is the simplest

You only need to do two things:

  • 1
    Build your page in Claude Use the prompt in Level 4 (Build a Page). Claude will give you a big block of text — that's your page's code!
  • 2
    Copy it and send it to Uncle B on WhatsApp Select all the code Claude gave you, copy it, and paste it into a WhatsApp message to Uncle B. He'll put it online and send you back your link — done! 🎉
💬 What to say: "Uncle B, here's my webpage code, can you put it online please?" — then paste the code underneath.
OR have a go yourself!
🚀

The Adventure Way — Do It Yourself

🔥 A little trickier — ask a parent to sit with you

Want to learn how to do it yourself? Here's how — with a grown-up helping.

We use a free website called GitHub. Think of it like a big filing cabinet on the internet. All the Code Quest files live in there already. When you add your file, it appears online automatically!

  • 1
    Build your page in Claude first Go to claude.ai and use this prompt:
Make me a fun personal webpage about me! My name is: [TYPE YOUR NAME HERE] My favourite colour is: [TYPE A COLOUR] I love: [TYPE YOUR HOBBY OR FAVOURITE THING] Make it colourful with big text and emojis. Show me the full HTML code.
👀 What you're looking for: Claude will write a LONG block of code. It starts with the words <!DOCTYPE html> at the very top. That whole thing is your webpage!
  • 2
    Copy ALL of the code Click anywhere on the code, then press Ctrl+A to select everything, then Ctrl+C to copy it. (On a Mac or iPad, use Cmd instead of Ctrl.)
  • 3
    Open the Code Quest filing cabinet Ask your parent to go to this website and log in — Uncle B has the password:
    github.com/altitudeaico/goteamolatoyes
  • 4
    Click the folder called "hackathon" You'll see a list of files and folders. Click the one called hackathon to open it — that's where your file will go.
  • 5
    Make a new file Click the button that says "Add file", then click "Create new file".
  • 6
    Give your file a name In the little box at the top, type your first name and then .html — like this:

    👧 elsie.html
    👦 theodore.html

    ⚠️ The .html bit at the end is really important — don't leave it out!
  • 7
    Paste your code into the big white box Click inside the big white area below the filename, then press Ctrl+V (or Cmd+V) to paste all your code in.
  • 8
    Click the green "Commit changes" button Scroll down to the bottom of the page and click the big green button. This saves your file into the filing cabinet — like pressing Save!
  • 9
    Wait about 1 minute... then visit your page! 🎉 Your page will be live on the internet at this address. Swap YOURNAME for whatever you named your file:

    altitudeaico.github.io/goteamolatoyes/hackathon/YOURNAME.html
🎉 Real example: If Elsie names her file elsie.html, her page will be at:
altitudeaico.github.io/goteamolatoyes/hackathon/elsie.html
She can share that link with anyone!
😬 Not working? Don't panic! The most common mistake is forgetting the .html at the end of the filename. If it's still not working after checking that, just send the code to Uncle B on WhatsApp and he'll sort it out.

Want to make your page even cooler?

No problem! Go back to Claude, ask it to make changes, then copy the new code and either send it to Uncle B or follow the steps above again to update your file.

Here are some fun things to try:

🎨 Change the colours and background:

Can you update my webpage so the background is dark with animated shooting stars? Use [YOUR FAVOURITE COLOUR] for the headings. Show me the full updated code.

⭐ Add a "My Favourite Things" section:

Add a section to my webpage called "My Top 5 Favourite Things" with: 1. [THING 1] 2. [THING 2] 3. [THING 3] 4. [THING 4] 5. [THING 5] Make it colourful with a big emoji next to each one. Show me the full updated code.
