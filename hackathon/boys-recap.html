Step by step

What We Did, Step by Step 🕹️

One evening. Three boys. Things went wrong, things got fixed, and by the end — a real quiz was live on the internet for anyone in the world to play!

🏎️
Ezekiel

Ezekiel Showed His Project! 🏎️

Ezekiel built a Race Engineer Simulator — like you're actually running an F1 race team! Uncle B looked at it and loved it. The only thing not working was the music, because Claude's preview screen can't play audio files. But the rest of it? Amazing.

💡
Team Decision

The Big Team Plan 💡

The boys came up with a great idea — instead of three separate quizzes, why not combine everyone's best ideas into ONE super quiz? Like assembling a superhero team! They decided to mix all their prompts together.

📧
Theodore & Arthur

Ezekiel Shared the Code 📧

Ezekiel emailed his code to Theodore and Arthur so they could all look at it together. That's exactly what real developers do — share their work with the team so everyone knows what's going on!

⚙️
Problem!

GitHub Said No! 😬

The boys set up their shared account (Tech Bros 3) and tried to save their code online. But GitHub said "not so fast!" — it needed a special secret key called a token, not just a normal password. Time to figure it out!

🔑
Win

Problem Solved! 🔑

Ezekiel created the special secret key (called a Personal Access Token), fixed a small typo in the address, and tried again. It worked! That's what real problem-solving looks like — don't give up, just keep trying!

🚀
HUGE WIN! 🎉

The Quiz Went Live on the Internet!

GitHub Pages switched on, and suddenly — their F1 quiz had a real link! Anyone in the whole world could type in that address and play their quiz. The Tech Bros just put something on the internet. That is SO cool. 🌍
