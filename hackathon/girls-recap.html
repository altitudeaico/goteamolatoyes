The Sessions

Two Sessions — Full Build Day 🛠️

The girls had the most productive day of the whole bootcamp. Two meetings on the same day — morning and evening — and they came out with a live, working quiz.

☀️

Session 1 — Elsie & Uncle B

Thursday 2nd April · 9:17am BST · 1-on-1 Build Session

Set up the full tech stack from scratch — GitHub for hosting, Supabase for the database. Both accounts live.
Team name confirmed: Girl Power. Brand identity locked in from the start.
Used Claude to design the quiz vision — pastel colours, glitter, sparkles, butterflies, confetti on correct answers.
Rejected Claude's first output for being too dark. Asked specifically for brighter blues, purples, fashion imagery and sparkles. That's product thinking.
Customised quiz topics to things Elsie actually knows and loves: fashion, art, makeup, cheerleading.
Action items: share credentials with Hannah, verify Hannah's email, update Claude thread description.
🌙

Session 2 — Full Girls Team

Thursday 2nd April · 5:55pm BST · Team Review Session

Audio chaos at the start — but they pushed through and got everyone connected. Classic remote teamwork.
Reviewed the quiz live — it was working and connected to the database. A fully functional multiplayer quiz.
Made a key product decision: the time limit was too short, so they eliminated it entirely. The quiz runs until everyone finishes.
Elsie tasked with final adjustments to both her quiz and the shared team version for Sophia and Hannah.
Highlights

The Best Moments 🌟

🎨

Elsie Rejected Claude

Claude's first quiz design came back too dark. Elsie didn't accept it — she went back with specific feedback: brighter colours, fashion imagery, sparkles. The second version was hers.

"Too dark. Make it brighter — blue and purple with fashion and sparkles." — Elsie's direction to Claude
✨

The Girl Power Vision

Pastel colours. Glitter. Butterflies. Confetti on correct answers. "Real sayings" as end messages. Elsie didn't just ask for a quiz — she designed an experience.

"Pastel colours, glitter, sparkles, butterfly themes and confetti animations." — The Girl Power brief
🗂️

Full Stack Setup

In one morning session, Elsie set up GitHub AND Supabase — the two technical pillars that make a real web app work. That's more than most adults have done.

"GitHub for hosting, Supabase for the database — both accounts live." — Session outcome
🔗

Connected to the Database

By the evening session, the quiz was live AND connected to Supabase. Real data being saved. Real multiplayer. That's not a demo — that's a working product.

"A functional quiz hosted and connected to the database was reviewed." — Session notes
⏱️

The Timer Decision

The team tested the quiz and felt the time limit was too short. So they killed it. That's a product decision made by a real user test — not just a gut feeling.

"Eliminate the time limit entirely — let the quiz run until players finish." — Team decision
